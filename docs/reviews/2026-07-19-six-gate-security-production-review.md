## Executive conclusion

The theorem is not closed and the implementation is not production-ready. The
repository has meaningful research evidence for exact `ExpandMask` computation:
the full 1,280-coefficient circuit matches a FIPS-oriented oracle, including a
two-party MAMA run at statistical security parameter 40. It also has a bounded
no-seed-dealer DKG/custody seam and an in-process partial-signing prototype that
emits a standard 3,309-byte ML-DSA-65 signature.

The signer patch now contains the correct algebraic seam for caller-supplied
additive `y_i`: each signer contribution weights only `s1_i`, and the resulting
`z_i` values are plain-summed. This resolves the former equation defect.

The components are still not composed into the requested production protocol.
The additive-mask API receives every signer-private share in one process and
explicitly reconstructs complete `y` for a direct check. The same signer starts
from one seed and materializes complete ML-DSA secret state; the DKG finalizer
can observe all clear shares; the custody vault is in-process; the 6,667
authorization identities are deterministically generated by one process; and
the campaign's negative cases are records rather than malicious protocol
executions. The latest campaign validator also reports no valid capture and zero
validated executions.

The immediate evidence-integrity issue remains: the campaign emitter asserts no
secret/seed reconstruction even though its invoked signer expands one local seed
into the full secret and currently collects/reconstructs all additive mask
shares. Additional campaign evidence is not trustworthy until that assertion is
derived from real process/custody evidence.

## Findings

### CRITICAL SEC-001: Campaign evidence contradicts the executed secret-reconstruction path

The campaign labels itself `actual_distributed_threshold_mldsa_campaign` and
sets `no_secret_or_seed_reconstruction = true`,
`secret_or_seed_reconstruction_used = false`, `simulation_used = false`, and
`fixture_harness_used = false` in
`/Users/rickglenn/Documents/lattice-threshold-backend-p1/src/internal_campaign.rs:19`,
`:23`, and `:210`.

The same emitter accepts one 32-byte seed, derives a per-case keygen seed, and
invokes `strict_distributed_sign_from_s1_y_partials` in the same process at
`/Users/rickglenn/Documents/lattice-threshold-backend-p1/src/internal_campaign.rs:449`.
That function calls `keygen_from_seed`, materializes complete `K`, `s1`, `s2`,
and `t0`, computes complete `rhopp` and `y`, and then manufactures all signer
shares locally in `src/backend/fips_sign.rs:436`, `:450`, `:468`, `:481`, and
`:525`.

The campaign validator requires the asserted no-reconstruction boolean and
rejects an asserted reconstruction boolean, but it does not establish the fact
from process, custody, or attestation evidence. See
`scripts/validate_internal_aggregation_campaign_capture.py:30` and `:349`.

Impact: once the other exact-MPC flags are wired, a centrally reconstructed run
could be misclassified as admissible distributed evidence. This is a theorem-
linkage integrity failure, not just missing production scale.

Required fix: make the current emitter declare reconstruction truthfully. A
production capture must derive the no-reconstruction state from authenticated
per-signer process/HSM attestations, DKG-to-vault import receipts, and protocol
messages, not from emitter-authored booleans.

### RESOLVED SEC-002: Additive mask partials now weight only `s1_i` and plain-sum `z_i`

The prior source-level equation defect is resolved. For additive MPC mask shares
and Shamir key shares, the required equation is:

```text
z_i = y_i + lambda_i * c * s1_i
z   = sum_i z_i
```

The patch implements that equation. `emit_additive_mask_partial` computes
`weighted_s1 = lambda_i * s1_i` before forming `z_i` at
`src/backend/fips_sign.rs:1420`, and `aggregate_additive_mask_partials` adds every
partial with scalar one at `src/backend/fips_sign.rs:1443`. Public `w_i = A*y_i`
commitments and additive mask shares are also plain-summed at `:1395` and
`:1482`. The supplied attempt is checked against the ordered signing-set binding
and retry counter at `:580` and `:593`.

Review disposition: **resolved in source review**. Tests were inspected but not
executed in this review. This disposition does not claim real MP-SPDZ output
ingestion, malicious security, no coordinator leakage, custody, production
scale, or theorem closure.

### CRITICAL SEC-011: The corrected additive seam still centralizes and reconstructs every private mask share

`strict_distributed_sign_from_additive_mask_outputs` accepts a slice of attempts,
each containing a vector of all signer-private `AdditiveMaskShare65` values. The
implementation clones the selected attempt's complete share vector and then
calls `aggregate_additive_mask_shares` to reconstruct full `y` in the signer
process at `src/backend/fips_sign.rs:593`, `:606`, and `:616`. The reconstructed
`y` is used in the direct aggregate check at `:634`.

The focused test computes full `ExpandMask` locally, splits it locally, and sets
`malicious_mpc_verified` and `exact_expandmask_equivalence_verified` to true in
ordinary structs at `src/backend/fips_sign.rs:2067`. It does not ingest private
outputs from distinct MP-SPDZ processes or establish that any share remained
private from the coordinator.

Impact: the algebra is correct, but the current integration API violates the
production no-joint-mask and no-single-observer boundary. Caller-supplied
verification booleans are not cryptographic evidence.

Required fix: each signer process must consume only its own private `y_i`, emit
only `w_i` and its own `z_i`, and erase `y_i` after one attempt. The coordinator
must never receive an all-shares container or reconstruct `y`. MPC verification,
input binding, output ownership, and retry consumption must be established by
authenticated transcript evidence rather than booleans supplied to the signer.

### HIGH SEC-003: No-single-holder DKG and production custody are not achieved

The DKG module states that the coordinator running `finalize`, or any holder of
`DkgOutput`, can reconstruct because it assembles all shares in clear. See
`src/crypto/mldsa_dkg.rs:12`. The bounded capture confirms
`coordinator_observed_clear_dkg_shares_before_custody = true`,
`process_isolated_receiver_custody = false`, and
`signer_consumes_custody_output = false` in
`artifacts/p1-dkg-custody-capture/latest/capture.json`.

The production signer also starts from one FIPS seed and does not consume the
DKG output. The current DKG covers research module-key contributions, but does
not jointly produce and retain the complete exact FIPS signing state, including
distributed `K` inputs for MPC `ExpandMask`, without a single holder.

Required fix: move share generation and decryption into distinct signer trust
domains; route accepted DKG outputs directly into those vaults; bind distributed
`K`, `s1`, `s2`, `t0`, `rho`, public key, and DKG transcript; and prohibit any
coordinator API that can collect threshold clear shares.

### HIGH SEC-004: DKG remains vulnerable to abort bias and lacks production attribution

The DKG's own boundary states that commit-before-reveal prevents adaptive
choice but not last-mover abort bias (`src/crypto/mldsa_dkg.rs:56`). Missing
reveals are silently excluded rather than producing attributable evidence, and
the in-memory frames are not signed (`:53` and `:62`). Complaint,
equivocation-across-receiver, accepted-set agreement, recovery, and anti-framing
semantics are not production complete.

Impact: a rushing dealer can influence the accepted dealer set by deciding
whether to reveal. Different observers or restart states can also lack a
non-repudiable basis for the same finalization decision.

Required fix: implement authenticated DKG frames, deterministic public
adjudication, signed finalization votes, missing-share recovery or a quantified
abort-bias bound, and a proof that every honest signer finalizes the same
accepted set and key.

### HIGH SEC-005: The 6,667 identities do not prove distinct custodial signers

The authorization certificate contains syntactically distinct validator IDs and
Ed25519 public keys, but all 6,667 signing keys are deterministically derived and
used inside one process from the request digest, committee size, and validator
ID. See
`/Users/rickglenn/Documents/lattice-threshold-backend-p1/src/internal_campaign.rs:571`
and `:678`.

The ML-DSA partials are likewise created in one call from one expanded secret,
not received from 6,667 mutually isolated custodians. Ed25519 authorization is
not evidence of 6,667 ML-DSA secret-share contributions.

Required fix: require one authenticated signer identity, custody attestation,
DKG receiver identity, MPC party identity, and ML-DSA partial receipt per
validator. Enforce uniqueness across those identities and bind every record to
the epoch, validator set, signing set, DKG transcript, key, message, and attempt.

### HIGH SEC-006: Malicious campaign cases are declarative, not executed attacks

For abort, malicious-share, rejection, and transcript-mutation cases,
`negative_case_transcript` constructs a JSON record with the requested outcome
without invoking a signer, network, malformed partial verifier, MPC party, or
abort/recovery state machine. See
`/Users/rickglenn/Documents/lattice-threshold-backend-p1/src/internal_campaign.rs:487`.

Impact: these records cannot demonstrate malicious security, rejection of a
malformed contribution, replay resistance, selective-abort handling, or
accountability.

Required fix: execute each negative case against the real distributed protocol,
capture sent/received authenticated frames, identify the rejecting component,
and bind the resulting fault or abort evidence to the immutable campaign.

### HIGH SEC-007: The current MAMA runner and network model cannot support a production 6,667-party claim

The runner starts every party as a local subprocess, uses local plaintext input
files, and supplies no host inventory, signer certificates, encrypted-channel
flag, remote orchestration, or attestation. See
`scripts/run_exact_expandmask_mpc_equivalence.py:88`, `:111`, and `:129`.

MP-SPDZ documentation says its protocols establish point-to-point connections
between every pair of parties, require one TCP port per party, and default to
unencrypted connections for dishonest-majority protocols. At 6,667 parties,
the pair graph contains 22,221,111 connections. The repository's two-party MAMA
run already reports about 3.269 GB aggregate traffic and roughly 18,000 rounds;
there is no measured or reviewed capacity model that justifies extrapolation to
6,667 parties.

MAMA provides malicious security with abort, not guaranteed output delivery.
The closure claim therefore also needs an availability and selective-abort
design for a malicious signer that terminates after learning protocol state.

Primary references:

- [MP-SPDZ repository and protocol/runtime boundary](https://github.com/data61/MP-SPDZ)
- [MP-SPDZ network setup](https://mp-spdz.readthedocs.io/en/v0.3.3/networking.html)
- [MP-SPDZ runtime encryption defaults](https://mp-spdz.readthedocs.io/en/latest/runtime-options.html)

Required fix: produce a reviewed topology and capacity model before scheduling
the campaign. Use authenticated encrypted channels and independently provisioned
party keys, prove distinct host/custodian identity, record every process and
connection, and demonstrate staged scale gates before 6,667 parties. If the
pairwise MAMA topology is infeasible, redesign the exact-mask service rather
than labeling a local process fan-out as a production run.

### HIGH SEC-008: Session, output, nonce, and replay binding are incomplete

The MPC circuit inputs XOR shares of `K` and `rnd`, accepts `mu` only from player
0, and emits raw private additive shares. It does not itself bind `sid`, epoch,
validator-set digest, DKG transcript, public key, exact signing set, attempt ID,
or output recipient attestation. See
`mpc/Programs/Source/mldsa65_expandmask.mpc:220`.

The custody transport explicitly states that `(key, nonce)` reuse is fatal and
leaves uniqueness to the caller (`src/crypto/share_transport.rs:31` and `:146`).
`seal_shared_secret_key` accepts a caller-provided `nonce_seed`, while vault
replay memory is in-process only (`src/crypto/receiver_custody.rs:27` and
`:349`). `CustodyContext` does not directly bind the canonical epoch, validator
set, DKG transcript, or dealer set (`src/crypto/receiver_custody.rs:57`).

Impact: rollback, restart, or coordinator substitution can replay a custody
envelope or mask output into an unintended ceremony or attempt. Reusing signing
masks across distinct challenges can expose secret-key information.

Required fix: use persistent monotonic attempt state, derive unique nonces inside
the custody boundary, bind every MPC input/output and partial to the complete
typed signing transcript, require every signer to verify `mu`, and erase or
irreversibly consume mask shares after one attempt, including rejected attempts.

### MEDIUM SEC-010: Test-scale MP-SPDZ evidence is not a reviewed production dependency

The two-party MAMA execution is useful test-scale malicious evidence, but
MP-SPDZ explicitly says its framework has not undergone the security review
needed for critical production code. The captured source commit and circuit
digests are necessary but insufficient for a production supply-chain claim.

Required fix: archive and reproduce the exact dependency source/build, produce
an SBOM and compiler/runtime configuration, review relevant MP-SPDZ protocol,
network, input, output, preprocessing, and persistence code, and bind the
reviewed binary digest to the campaign.
